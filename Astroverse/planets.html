Explore The Solar System

Journey across all eight planets and discover the wonders, mysteries, and scientific facts of our cosmic neighborhood.

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and also the smallest planet in our solar system.

  • No atmosphere to retain heat
  • Extreme temperature changes
  • A year lasts only 88 Earth days
  • Surface filled with impact craters

Venus

Venus is often called Earth’s twin because of its similar size, but it has the hottest surface in the solar system.

  • Covered with toxic clouds
  • Rotates backwards compared to most planets
  • Surface temperature can melt lead
  • Dense carbon dioxide atmosphere

🌍 Earth

Earth is the only known planet that supports life and contains liquid water on its surface.

  • 71% of surface covered with water
  • Protected by magnetic field
  • Home to millions of species
  • Perfect distance from the Sun

Mars

Mars is known as the Red Planet due to iron oxide dust covering its surface.

  • Home to Olympus Mons volcano
  • Evidence of ancient water flows
  • Target for future human missions
  • Has two small moons

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system and is made mostly of gas.

  • Famous Great Red Spot storm
  • Has over 90 known moons
  • Extremely strong magnetic field
  • Could fit 1,300 Earths inside

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its beautiful ring system made of ice and rock particles.

  • Second largest planet
  • Has hundreds of ring segments
  • Very low density planet
  • More than 140 moons discovered

Uranus

Uranus is an icy giant that rotates sideways compared to other planets.

  • Blue-green color from methane gas
  • Very cold atmosphere
  • Has faint ring system
  • Takes 84 Earth years to orbit the Sun

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest known planet from the Sun and experiences powerful storms.

  • Fastest winds in the solar system
  • Deep blue methane atmosphere
  • Discovered using mathematics
  • One orbit takes 165 Earth years
